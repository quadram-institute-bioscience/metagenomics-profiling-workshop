title: FastQC
---

[FastQC](https://www.bioinformatics.babraham.ac.uk/projects/fastqc/) is a tool to perform quality control checks on raw sequence data coming from high throughput sequencing pipelines. It provides a modular set of analyses which you can use to give a quick impression of whether your data has any problems of which you should be aware before doing any further analysis.

FastQC does not alter or clean your data, it simply provides a report to help you assess the quality of your data. It is a good idea to run FastQC on your raw data before doing any further analysis, and also after any filtering or trimming steps to see how the quality has changed.

There is a widespread lie about bioinformatics methods: "QC of raw reads was performed using FastQC". The issue is that what actions were taken after viewing the report are usually not reported...

The semaphores system can be misleading: it is a summary and it was designed with genome shotgun sequencing in mind.

For example, when performing a metabarcoding experiment, it is *expected* to have a strong bias in the base composition of the reads, and this will trigger a warning in the report. This is not a problem, it is simply a property of the data.
